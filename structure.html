Welcome to the Structure page


There are many different musical instruments that make up an orchestra. An orchestra's conductor directs the musicians and keeps the beat of the music. He or she is in charge of making sure the musicians play in unison. The strings, woodwinds, brass, and percussion are the orchestra's four principal groups.

The APU orchestra band is made of the following sections:

String Instruments:

  • 1st Violins with approximately 18 players
  • 2nd Violins with approximately 16 players
  • Violas with approximately 12 players
  • Cellos with approximately 10 players
  • Double basses with 8 approximately players




Woodwind Instruments:

  • Piccolo – normally 1
  • Flute – normally 2, sometimes 3
  • Clarinet – normally 2, sometimes 3
  • Oboe – normally 2, sometimes 3
  • Bassoon – normally 2 or 3
  • 1 Contrabassoon
  • 1 English Horn




Brass Instruments:

  • Basically 3-4 Trumpets
  • Basically 3-6 French Horns
  • Basically 2-3 Trombones
  • 1 Cimbasso
  • 1 Tuba




Percussion Instruments:

  • Cymbals
  • Snare drum
  • Bass drum
  • Timpani
  • Xylophone
  • Glockenspiel
